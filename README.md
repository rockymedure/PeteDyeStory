# Pete Dye Golf Club Story

A curated archive of Jimmy LaRosa's Substack documenting the remarkable 18-year journey to build the Pete Dye Golf Club in the hills of West Virginia.

## About

While developing the Pete Dye Golf Club, they discovered 250 years of history buried in the WV hills—Indian moccasins, miners' boots, and golfers' spikes. This collection preserves the stories of the people, setbacks, and triumphs that brought this legendary course to life.

**Original Source:** [Jimmy LaRosa's Substack](https://jimmylarosa.substack.com)

## The Story

The Pete Dye Golf Club story spans decades of determination:

- **The Vision** — A dream to build a world-class golf course in West Virginia
- **18 Years of Construction** — Overcoming ice storms, financial challenges, and Pete Dye's perfectionism
- **1994-95** — The turning point that changed everything
- **Grand Opening** — National recognition, Links Magazine features, and the arrival of tournaments
- **Legacy** — Hosting the WV State Amateur and 2000 WV Open

## About Pete Dye

Pete Dye (1925–2020) was one of golf's most influential course architects, known for designs that challenge and inspire. His courses include TPC Sawgrass, Whistling Straits, and the Ocean Course at Kiawah Island. The Pete Dye Golf Club in Bridgeport, West Virginia stands as a testament to his vision and the dedication of those who spent nearly two decades bringing it to life.
