# Where We Are and Where We're Going

Jimmy, Seth — what we're prioritizing is the story of how this happened. Not the golf course as it is today. Not the beauty shots. Not the reflections. The story. Beginning to end. How two coal miners convinced the greatest golf architect alive to stake his legacy on an abandoned mine — and then spent 18 years proving he was right to do it.

The rough cut is beautiful, and there's real footage in it we'll use. But it's a portrait of the place, not the story of the place. What we're building is the story — told clearly enough that anyone can follow it, scene by scene, from the scarred land to the bell on 12.

---

## Our approach to footage

The story comes first. We will not skip a scene or weaken the narrative because we're missing a shot.

- If we have the footage — archival or new — we use it.
- If we don't, we generate it. AI-assisted video generation, faithful to every detail in the interviews, the banquet tapes, the construction footage, the transcripts, and the family's own accounts. Every generated frame will be grounded in real reference material. Nothing invented. Nothing embellished.

The 31 archival videos, 180+ extracted clips, full transcripts, and decades of documentation give us a deep well of reference. Where footage exists, we use it. Where it doesn't, we have everything we need to recreate it faithfully.

---

**What's changing in the film:**

The opening flips. No more beauty shots and aerials first. It opens on the scarred land, the orange water, the coal. James D.'s voice. Then Papa Jim. The audience needs to understand what this place *was* before they see what it became.

The middle of the film right now says the family faced "every conceivable challenge" for 18 years — but it doesn't show a single one. We're adding the real stories: Papa Jim nearly dying from black damp in the mine shaft on 13. Three near-foreclosures. The Storm of the Century burying the back nine. The lake on 15 that leaked twice. The Lupton loan that got rescinded and then saved by a letter.

The bell on 12 has to be in the film. It's the emotional climax — Pete ringing a bell in memory of his father, on a course he spent 18 years building with another father and son. We search the archive for it. If it's there, we use it. If it's not, we generate it — faithful to the moment as it's been described by everyone who was there.

The closing: Pete's final line about the property, a drone pulling back over all 18 holes at sunrise, the bell sound, and then white text on black: *"Pete Dye designed over 100 golf courses. He put his name on one."*

## Why This Version Matters for Sponsors

West Virginia tourism, the coal association, economic development — they don't need a golf commercial. They need a story that proves what working-class West Virginians can build when they refuse to quit. The LaRosa family story does that better than anything else in the state.

This version of the film leads with the heritage, the coal, the immigrant roots, and the grit — and then delivers the proof that the whole world validated it. That's what opens doors.

## The Film in 11 Beats

1. **The Land** — Open on the scarred hills, the coal, the ruin. James D.'s voice over the images.
2. **The Dreamer** — Papa Jim. The builder. The wild idea to put a golf course on coal country.
3. **Pete Dye** — 45 seconds. Sawgrass, Kiawah, Whistling Straits. So the audience knows who said no.
4. **The Rejection** — "Call it Miracle Mountain — it'll be a miracle if anybody wanted to play it."
5. **The Second Chance** — The mine at Simpson Creek. Pete walks in with flags, comes out grinning. A handshake. 1978.
6. **The Long Middle** — 18 years. The partnership, the crises, the team, Alice. This is the guts of it. The section that makes the audience feel what the family endured.
7. **The Naming** — Pete on the 6th fairway: "The hell with it. Let's call it Pete Dye." And the line that changes everything: "This will be the only club named after me."
8. **Opening Day** — July 4, 1993. 1994. July 4, 1995. Bagpipes. Two governors. Joe DiMaggio.
9. **The Bell** — Pete rings the bell on 12 in memory of his father. Three fathers, three sons. The climax.
10. **The Proof** — Top 100. #2 among 9,000. 474 members, 27 states, 5 countries. Quick. The proof isn't the point — it's what makes the improbability undeniable.
11. **The Land** — Drone at sunrise over all 18 holes. The same ground, transformed. Bell sound. Title card.
