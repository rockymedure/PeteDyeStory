## What We're Doing

The current cut is a beautiful portrait of the golf course as it is today. What we need is the story of how it got there. If someone who's never heard of Pete Dye watches this film, they should be able to tell you exactly what happened, in order, by the time it's over.

Your footage is in this film. The beauty is in this film. But it comes at the end — as the reward — not at the beginning.

**The story comes first.** We will not weaken the narrative because a shot is missing. If we have the footage — archival or from your cut — we use it. If we don't, we generate it with AI video, faithful to the interviews, banquet tapes, construction footage, and family accounts. Every generated frame grounded in real reference material. Nothing invented.

### What needs to change

1. **Open on devastation, not beauty.** Scarred land, coal, ruin. Save the beauty for the end.
2. **Add 45 seconds of "Who Is Pete Dye?"** before the rejection. The audience needs to know what his name means.
3. **Expand the middle.** 5 minutes of generic struggle becomes 5–7 minutes of specific crises — the near-death, the storms, the money, the lake.
4. **Move hospitality and gates to the closing.** They're payoff. They don't belong before Opening Day.
5. **Add Opening Day with dates.** July 4, 1993. 1994. July 4, 1995. Clear supers.
6. **Add the bell on #12.** The climax. Without it, the film has no peak.
7. **Rework the closing:** "Our spot" → Pete's line → drone sunrise → bell → title card.
8. **Lower thirds.** The audience needs to know who Jimmy is by minute 2.

---

## The Problem with the Current Cut

**It doesn't tell the story.** The cut is a portrait of the golf course as it exists today — beautiful footage, people reflecting on how special the place is. But if you showed it to someone who's never heard of Pete Dye and asked "what happened?", they couldn't tell you. They'd know the place is beautiful and a lot of people love it. They wouldn't know how it got there.

The story — the rejection, the second chance, the 18 years of crises, the naming, the bell — is either missing or buried. Specifically:

1. **The narrative isn't there.** The film doesn't take the audience through what happened, step by step, beginning to end. It reflects on the place instead of telling the story of the place.

2. **The emotional climax is missing entirely.** The bell on #12 — Pete ringing it on Grand Opening morning in memory of his father — isn't in the cut. Not a frame, not a word.

3. **Payoff material is spent too early.** Hospitality (20:00–22:30) and the gates sequence (22:30–25:00) belong after Opening Day, not before.

### Scene 9: The Bell
**Target:** 1:00–1:30

**Not in the cut.** The single most important addition.

The bell on #12 was installed in memory of Pete's father — the man who built Pete's first nine holes. On Grand Opening morning, Pete saw it for the first time and rang it.

Three fathers. Three sons:
- James D. came to America. His son Papa Jim dreamed of building a golf course.
- Papa Jim and Jimmy spent 18 years refusing to quit.
- Pete's father built Pete's first nine holes. The bell honors him.

This is the emotional climax.

**Search:** `PDGC Grand Opening`, `PDGC Opening 7-3-1993` for footage of Pete at #12.

**If not in the archive:** Generate it — AI video faithful to the archival look and feel. Morning light, bell close-up, a hand reaching for it, the ring. Jimmy's existing audio layered over.

**Sound:** Third bell tone — full, resonant. Hold. The faint bell from the opening finally resolves.
